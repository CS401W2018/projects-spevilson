Hello, my name is Spencer Wilson

Here's a short introduction:
I was born in Spokane Washington as the firstborn son of my mom and dad. As a kid, my dad
and I would do math problems, learn about animals, and eat Doritos. I grew up being best friends
with my brother Jay, who is two years younger than me. After a while, two more brothers came,
and they are awesome.
I was baptized at 8 years old, and when I was 11½ we moved to be on the same street as the
Spokane Temple. I loved going to the temple then, and I also liked biking to Albertsons for
donuts. Some interests and hobbies that I have had since I was a child are:

  1. Eating
  2. Cooking
  3. Playing music
  4. Building and inventing
  5. Star Wars
  6. Reading books
  7. Doing math

I served my mission in Thailand and met my wife there. She is from Thailand, but also served as
a missionary there. We were married in August if last year, and I moved here to be with her.

To elaborate on my list, my favorite things to eat are steak, burgers, Thai food, pasta, and
chocolate milk. I also really like to cook Thai food and pasta, but my favorite thing to cook is
new things that I make up on the spot. I used to be in a garage rock band playing electric guitar,
and I really like rock music. The most important thing to me is the gospel of Jesus Christ.
I have a testimony of Him and His love for me, and I will never deny that testimony. I love the
Book of Mormon, and the habits from my mission will always stay with me. One of my favorite
scriptures to think about recently:

"For the natural man is an enemy to God, and has been from the fall of Adam, and
will be, forever and ever, unless he yields to the enticings of the Holy Spirit, and
putteth off the natural man and becometh a saint through the atonement of Christ the
Lord, and becometh as a child, submissive, meek, humble, patient, full of love,
willing to submit to all things which the Lord seeth fit to inflict upon him, even as a
child doth submit to his father."

           -King Benjamin

I think that something the Lord has tried to teach me in my life is patience, and how to submit to His will, like the Savior submitted to the Father's will.

Thanks for reading.
